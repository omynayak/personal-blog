Hello there, (hopefully) friendly passerby!

Welcome to Om's Home—yeah, I know, kind of a cliché name, but it works for now. This is my corner of the internet where I share bits of who I am—or maybe just a vague, abstract concept of a being, if that’s more your vibe.

Man, who even are you?

My name is Om. I like building things. I also love dogs (I have eight!) and cats, and I’ve always been drawn to the ocean. If that resonates with you, feel free to head over to the gallery.

More seriously, I’m a 20-year-old engineering student at a small college in India. I spend most of my time chasing novelty—whether it's through new ideas, obscure topics, or just interesting stuff to learn. When I’m not tinkering at my desk, you’ll probably find me in the garden admiring the flowers, lounging with a book, or watching something in the living room.

Recently, I stumbled into the world of web development. For someone who mostly writes low-level systems code and still thinks of strings as char*, it felt like sorcery—and a ton of fun. So like every overconfident nerd before me, I said, “Meh, how hard could it be?” and dove headfirst into The Odin Project, an incredibly cool (and arguably the best) place to start learning web development.

As I hacked my way through the dense jungle of web dev, I got excited by the idea of making a site for myself—a place to show off the nifty things I build, share thoughts, and document random cool discoveries. Basically, it’s a way to stop spamming my friends with a hundred texts a day—and maybe even make some new ones along the way!
